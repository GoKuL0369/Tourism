Welcome To Kasargod

The northernmost district of Kerala, Kasaragod is famous for its handlooms and its 293 kilometre long coastline. With its cultural uniqueness, the place is known as the land of gods. Rich in history, Kasaragod is home to the largest and best preserved fort in the State - Bekal.

place to explore in kasargod

Ranipuram

Ranipuram is a village and a major tourist attraction in the Kasaragod district of the Indian state of Kerala. It is located near Talakaveri Wildlife Sanctuary in Kerala-Karnataka border.

Bekkal fort

Bekal Fort was built by Shivappa Nayaka of Keladi in 1650 AD, at Bekal. It is the largest fort in Kerala, spreading over 40 acres.

Azhithala Beach

Situated 25kms south of Bekal, Azhithala is a place where Tejaswini River mouths the Arabian Sea. Calm and secluded, Azhithala beach is scattered with casuarina trees and is an ideal place for relaxation and long walks.
